2. YOUR FIRST DAY
================================================================================

2.1 What to Expect

YOUR SCHEDULE:

9:00 AM - Arrival and Check-In
- Meet your onboarding buddy at reception
- Complete HR paperwork (if not done online)
- Receive employee badge and welcome kit

9:30 AM - IT Setup
- Receive laptop, phone, and equipment
- Set up accounts and passwords
- Configure email and essential tools

10:30 AM - Office Tour
- Learn building layout and facilities
- Meet office manager and key personnel
- Locate your desk/workspace

11:00 AM - Welcome Meeting with Manager
- Review role expectations
- Discuss immediate priorities
- Set up regular 1:1 meetings

12:00 PM - Team Lunch (On Us!)
- Informal lunch with your immediate team
- Get to know your colleagues
- Ask questions in relaxed setting

1:30 PM - HR Orientation
- Benefits overview
- Company policies and procedures
- Complete required training modules

3:00 PM - Department Introduction
- Meet broader team
- Learn about current projects
- Understand team dynamics

4:00 PM - First Assignments
- Review initial tasks from manager
- Set up development environment
- Begin familiarization with codebase/systems

5:00 PM - Day 1 Wrap-Up
- Recap with onboarding buddy
- Address any immediate questions
- Plan for Day 2

2.2 What to Bring

REQUIRED:
✓ Government-issued photo ID
✓ Social Security card or passport
✓ Bank account info (for direct deposit)
✓ Signed offer letter and paperwork
✓ Voided check (for payroll)

OPTIONAL:
✓ Lunch or snacks
✓ Water bottle
✓ Notebook and pen
✓ Personal items for your desk

2.3 Dress Code

TechCorp dress code: Business Casual

ACCEPTABLE:
- Jeans (clean, no rips)
- Casual shirts, blouses
- Sneakers, casual shoes
- Hoodies and sweatshirts (TechCorp branded preferred)

AVOID:
- Offensive graphics or slogans
- Beachwear or athletic wear
- Excessive revealing clothing

Client-facing meetings: Dress one level up (business professional)

2.4 First Day Checklist

By end of Day 1, you should have:

□ Employee badge
□ Laptop and charger
□ Company phone (if applicable)
□ Email account set up
□ Slack/Teams account active
□ Building and office access working
□ Emergency contact info submitted
□ Direct deposit enrolled
□ Benefits enrollment started
□ Desk/workspace assigned

3. YOUR FIRST WEEK
================================================================================

3.1 Week 1 Goals

By end of your first week, you should:

ADMINISTRATIVE:
□ Complete all HR onboarding tasks
□ Finish required training modules
□ Set up benefits elections
□ Set up 1:1 meetings with key stakeholders

TECHNICAL:
□ Access all necessary systems and tools
□ Set up development environment
□ Review relevant documentation
□ Make first small contribution (bug fix, doc update)

RELATIONSHIP BUILDING:
□ Meet your direct team members
□ Schedule coffee chats with 3-5 colleagues
□ Attend team meetings as observer
□ Join relevant Slack channels

LEARNING:
□ Understand company mission and values
□ Learn about your team's projects
□ Review codebase or relevant materials
□ Identify knowledge gaps and learning resources

3.2 Key Meetings

DAILY:
- Team standup (15 min)
- Check-in with onboarding buddy (informal)

WEEK 1:
- 1:1 with direct manager (60 min)
- Team introduction meeting (30 min)
- HR orientation session (2 hours)
- IT systems training (1 hour)
- Benefits enrollment session (30 min)

OPTIONAL:
- Coffee chats with team members
- Lunch with different colleagues each day
- Attend company all-hands if scheduled

3.3 First Week Training Modules

Complete in online learning portal:

REQUIRED (Must complete in Week 1):
□ Information Security Basics (2 hours)
□ Code of Conduct and Ethics (1 hour)
□ Anti-Harassment Training (1 hour)
□ Data Privacy Fundamentals (1 hour)
□ Emergency Procedures (30 min)

RECOMMENDED:
□ Company History and Values (30 min)
□ Product Overview (45 min)
□ Tools and Systems Overview (1 hour)

3.4 First Week Tips

✓ Take notes - lots of information coming your way
✓ Ask questions - there are no stupid questions
✓ Don't try to know everything at once
✓ Make connections - introduce yourself proactively
✓ Observe team dynamics and culture
✓ Keep normal working hours - don't overdo it
✓ Use your onboarding buddy liberally
✓ Review materials shared during meetings

4. YOUR FIRST MONTH
================================================================================

4.1 30-Day Goals

PERFORMANCE:
□ Complete first significant project or task
□ Demonstrate understanding of role responsibilities
□ Begin contributing to team objectives
□ Receive feedback from manager

INTEGRATION:
□ Establish relationships with key stakeholders
□ Attend all relevant team meetings
□ Join at least one employee resource group or interest group
□ Participate in company social events

DEVELOPMENT:
□ Complete all onboarding training
□ Identify learning and development goals
□ Schedule quarterly goal-setting meeting with manager
□ Begin working on 90-day objectives

4.2 30-Day Check-In

Your manager will conduct a formal 30-day check-in to:
- Review progress and accomplishments
- Discuss challenges and roadblocks
- Clarify expectations
- Adjust goals if needed
- Provide feedback
- Answer questions

Prepare for this meeting by:
- Reflecting on your first month
- Documenting accomplishments
- Identifying questions or concerns
- Thinking about your 90-day goals

4.3 90-Day Plan

Work with your manager to create a 90-day plan that includes:

LEARNING OBJECTIVES:
- Technical skills to acquire
- Product knowledge to gain
- Processes to master
- Tools to learn

PERFORMANCE OBJECTIVES:
- Specific deliverables
- Projects to complete
- Contributions to make
- Metrics to achieve

RELATIONSHIP OBJECTIVES:
- Stakeholders to build relationships with
- Cross-functional connections to make
- Mentorship opportunities
- Team collaboration goals

4.4 Probationary Period

Your first 90 days are a probationary period:
- Performance will be evaluated at 30, 60, and 90 days
- Opportunity to ensure mutual fit
- Termination is possible if expectations not met
- Most employees successfully complete probation

Success factors:
✓ Deliver on commitments
✓ Communicate proactively
✓ Seek feedback and act on it
✓ Demonstrate cultural fit
✓ Show growth mindset
✓ Collaborate effectively

9. CAREER DEVELOPMENT
================================================================================

9.1 Growth Pathways

TechCorp supports two career tracks:

INDIVIDUAL CONTRIBUTOR (IC):
Junior → Mid-Level → Senior → Staff → Principal → Distinguished

MANAGEMENT:
Team Lead → Manager → Senior Manager → Director → VP → SVP → C-Level

You can switch tracks based on interests and strengths.

9.2 Performance Reviews

FREQUENCY:
- Quarterly check-ins
- Annual formal review

PROCESS:
1. Self-assessment
2. Manager assessment
3. Peer feedback (360-degree)
4. Calibration across teams
5. Review meeting with manager
6. Goal setting for next period

9.3 Promotion Process

Promotions based on:
- Demonstrated performance at next level
- Business impact
- Leadership and collaboration
- Consistent excellence over time

Timeline:
- Typically 18-24 months between promotions
- Faster for exceptional performance
- Discussed during annual review

9.4 Learning Resources

INTERNAL:

EXTERNAL:

9.5 Internal Mobility

- Move between teams

10. FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
================================================================================

Q: When do I get paid?
A: Bi-weekly on Fridays. First paycheck will be on the second payday after
   your start date.

Q: How do I request time off?
A: Submit requests via HR Portal at least 2 weeks in advance. Manager approval
   required.

Q: What if I'm sick?
A: Notify your manager ASAP. No doctor's note needed for 1-3 days.

Q: Can I work remotely?
A: Depends on your role. Discuss with your manager. See Remote Work Policy.

Q: How do I expense something?
A: Submit via Concur Expense portal with receipts. See Expense Policy.

Q: When are performance reviews?
A: Quarterly check-ins, annual formal review (typically in January).

Q: How do I get promoted?
A: Consistent performance at next level, discussed during annual review.

Q: Can I refer someone for a job?
A: Yes! $3,000 bonus for successful referrals. Use careers portal.

Q: What if I have a conflict with a colleague?
A: Talk to them first. If unresolved, involve your manager or HR.

Q: Who do I contact for IT issues?
A: IT Helpdesk: helpdesk@techcorp.com or (555) 123-5555

Q: How do I book a conference room?
A: Via Outlook calendar. Search "Conference Rooms" and add to meeting.

Q: Can I bring guests to the office?
A: Yes, but they must sign in and be escorted. Notify reception in advance.

Q: What's the remote work policy?
A: Varies by role. Generally 2-3 days remote per week allowed. See policy.

Q: How do I join employee resource groups?
A: Check Slack channels or contact diversity@techcorp.com
