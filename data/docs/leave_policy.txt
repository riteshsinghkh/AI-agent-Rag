1. OVERVIEW
================================================================================

This policy outlines the leave entitlements and procedures for all employees
of TechCorp Inc. It is designed to ensure fair and consistent treatment while
supporting work-life balance.

Scope: This policy applies to all full-time and part-time employees.
Contractors and interns have separate leave arrangements.

Policy Owner: Human Resources Department
Contact: hr@techcorp.com | Extension: 5500

2. TYPES OF LEAVE
================================================================================

TechCorp offers the following types of leave:

a) Annual Leave / Paid Time Off (PTO)
b) Sick Leave
c) Parental Leave (Maternity/Paternity/Adoption)
d) Bereavement Leave
e) Public Holidays
f) Unpaid Leave (special circumstances)
g) Jury Duty / Civic Duty Leave

================================================================================
3. ANNUAL LEAVE / PAID TIME OFF (PTO)
================================================================================

3.1 Entitlement

Full-time employees are entitled to annual leave based on tenure:

- Years 0-2: 15 days per year
- Years 3-5: 20 days per year
- Years 6-10: 25 days per year
- Years 10+: 30 days per year

Part-time employees receive pro-rated leave based on their working hours.

3.2 Accrual

Annual leave accrues monthly at a rate of 1/12th of the annual entitlement.
Leave can be taken as soon as it accrues; there is no waiting period.

3.3 Carry Forward

Up to 5 days of unused annual leave may be carried forward to the next year.
Any leave exceeding this limit will be forfeited unless approved by management
for exceptional circumstances.

3.4 Cash Out

Employees may cash out up to 5 days of accrued leave per year, subject to:
- Manager approval
- Minimum balance of 10 days remaining after cash out
- Request submitted by November 30th

3.5 Leave During Notice Period

Employees are generally not permitted to take annual leave during their notice
period. Exceptions require approval from the HR Director.

================================================================================
4. SICK LEAVE
================================================================================

4.1 Entitlement

All employees are entitled to 10 days of paid sick leave per year.
Sick leave does not accrue and resets annually on January 1st.

4.2 Usage

Sick leave can be used for:
- Personal illness or injury
- Medical appointments
- Caring for immediate family members who are ill
- Mental health days

4.3 Medical Certificate

A medical certificate is required for:
- Sick leave exceeding 3 consecutive days
- Sick leave taken immediately before or after a public holiday or annual leave
- Patterns of frequent short-term absences (as determined by HR)

4.4 Notification

Employees must notify their direct manager:
- As early as possible on the first day of absence
- By 9:00 AM if possible
- Via phone call or email to manager and HR

4.5 Unused Sick Leave

Unused sick leave does not carry forward and cannot be cashed out.

================================================================================
5. PARENTAL LEAVE
================================================================================

5.1 Maternity Leave

Eligible employees are entitled to:
- 18 weeks of paid maternity leave (100% of base salary)
- Additional 8 weeks unpaid leave (optional)

Eligibility: Must have been employed for at least 12 months

5.2 Paternity Leave

Eligible employees are entitled to:
- 4 weeks of paid paternity leave (100% of base salary)
- To be taken within 6 months of the birth or adoption

5.3 Adoption Leave

Same entitlements as maternity/paternity leave apply to adoptive parents.

5.4 Notification

Employees must notify HR at least 8 weeks before the expected leave start date
and provide relevant documentation.

5.5 Return to Work

Employees are guaranteed to return to the same or equivalent position.
Flexible return arrangements can be discussed with HR and management.

================================================================================
6. BEREAVEMENT LEAVE
================================================================================

6.1 Entitlement

Employees are entitled to paid bereavement leave for the death of:

Immediate Family (Spouse, Child, Parent, Sibling):
- 5 days paid leave

Extended Family (Grandparent, In-law, Aunt, Uncle):
- 3 days paid leave

Other Circumstances:
- 1 day paid leave (at manager's discretion)

6.2 Additional Leave

Additional unpaid leave may be granted at the discretion of management.

6.3 Documentation

A death certificate or funeral notice may be requested.

7. PUBLIC HOLIDAYS
================================================================================

7.1 Recognized Holidays

TechCorp observes the following public holidays:

- New Year's Day
- Martin Luther King Jr. Day
- Presidents' Day
- Memorial Day
- Independence Day
- Labor Day
- Thanksgiving Day
- Day After Thanksgiving
- Christmas Eve
- Christmas Day
- New Year's Eve

Total: 11 days per year

7.2 Holiday Pay

Full-time employees receive 100% pay for public holidays.
Part-time employees receive pro-rated holiday pay.

7.3 Working on Holidays

If business needs require work on a public holiday:
- Employees receive time-and-a-half pay
- Plus a day off in lieu within 30 days

8. LEAVE APPLICATION PROCESS
================================================================================

8.1 How to Apply

All leave requests must be submitted through the HR Portal:
https://portal.techcorp.com/leave

8.2 Advance Notice

Minimum advance notice required:

- 1-3 days leave: 1 week notice
- 4-10 days leave: 2 weeks notice
- 10+ days leave: 4 weeks notice
- Emergency/sick leave: As soon as possible

8.3 Required Information

Leave requests must include:
- Type of leave
- Start and end dates
- Total number of days
- Reason (for certain leave types)
- Contact information during leave (optional)

8.4 Approval Workflow

1. Employee submits request via HR Portal
2. Direct Manager reviews and approves/denies
3. HR validates leave balance and policy compliance
4. Employee receives confirmation email

================================================================================
9. LEAVE APPROVAL GUIDELINES
================================================================================

9.1 Manager Considerations

When reviewing leave requests, managers should consider:
- Business needs and workload
- Team coverage
- Prior leave commitments
- First-come, first-served principle (for annual leave)
- Employee tenure and performance

9.2 Denial of Leave

Leave may be denied if:
- Inadequate notice provided
- Critical business period (e.g., year-end, product launch)
- Insufficient team coverage
- Excessive leave already taken

If leave is denied, the manager must:
- Provide written explanation
- Suggest alternative dates
- Escalate to HR if employee disputes the decision

9.3 Blackout Periods

Certain departments may designate blackout periods where leave is restricted:
- Finance: December 15 - January 15 (year-end close)
- Sales: Last week of each quarter
- IT: During major system upgrades

Employees will be notified of blackout periods at least 60 days in advance.

10. FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
================================================================================

Q: Can I take leave in half-day increments?
A: Yes, leave can be taken in half-day (4-hour) increments for annual and
   sick leave. Minimum increment is 0.5 days.

Q: What happens to my leave if I go on long-term disability?
A: Leave accrual is suspended during long-term disability. Contact HR for
   specific guidance.

Q: Can I donate leave to a colleague?
A: TechCorp does not currently have a leave donation program. This is under
   review for future implementation.

Q: What if I'm sick while on annual leave?
A: If you are hospitalized or seriously ill during annual leave, you may
   convert those days to sick leave with a medical certificate. Contact HR
   immediately.

Q: How do I check my leave balance?
A: Log in to the HR Portal at https://portal.techcorp.com/leave
   Your current balance is displayed on the dashboard.

Q: Can I take unpaid leave?
A: Unpaid leave may be granted for exceptional circumstances (e.g., extended
   travel, personal reasons). Submit a request through your manager and HR
   at least 8 weeks in advance.

Q: What if my leave request is urgent/emergency?
A: Contact your manager immediately by phone. Follow up with a formal
   request in the HR Portal as soon as possible.

Q: Are there any restrictions on when I can take leave?
A: Yes, subject to business needs, blackout periods, and team coverage.
   First-come, first-served generally applies for peak periods like
   summer and December holidays.
