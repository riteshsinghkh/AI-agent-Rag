1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE
================================================================================

This policy establishes guidelines for employee expense reimbursement to ensure:
- Consistent and fair treatment of all employees
- Proper use of company funds
- Compliance with tax regulations and accounting standards
- Timely reimbursement for legitimate business expenses

This policy applies to all TechCorp employees, contractors, and authorized
third parties who incur business-related expenses.

Policy Owner: Finance Department
Contact: expenses@techcorp.com | Extension: 5400

================================================================================
2. ELIGIBLE EXPENSES
================================================================================

TechCorp will reimburse reasonable business expenses incurred on behalf of
the company. Expenses must be:

- Necessary for business purposes
- Reasonable in amount
- Properly documented
- Pre-approved when required
- Submitted within 30 days of incurrence

2.1 Common Eligible Expenses

TRAVEL:
✓ Airfare (economy class)
✓ Ground transportation (taxi, rideshare, rental car)
✓ Lodging (reasonable hotel rates)
✓ Meals during business travel
✓ Parking and tolls

OFFICE SUPPLIES:
✓ Stationery, pens, notebooks
✓ Printer supplies
✓ Office organization items
✓ Basic desk accessories

PROFESSIONAL DEVELOPMENT:
✓ Conference registration fees
✓ Professional memberships (job-related)
✓ Training courses and certifications
✓ Industry publications and books

TECHNOLOGY:
✓ Software subscriptions (approved)
✓ Mobile phone (job-related usage)
✓ Internet service (remote workers)
✓ Peripherals and accessories (approved)

CLIENT-RELATED:
✓ Client meals and entertainment
✓ Client gifts (within limits)
✓ Meeting room rentals
✓ Presentation materials

MISCELLANEOUS:
✓ Business mileage (personal vehicle)
✓ Home office setup (remote workers, one-time)
✓ Shipping and courier services
✓ Bank fees for business transactions

3. NON-REIMBURSABLE EXPENSES
================================================================================

The following expenses will NOT be reimbursed:

PERSONAL EXPENSES:
✗ Personal meals (not during travel)
✗ Personal shopping or entertainment
✗ Personal phone calls (except brief check-ins)
✗ Gym memberships or fitness
✗ Personal grooming (haircuts, spa, etc.)
✗ Commuting to regular office location

EXCESSIVE OR LUXURY EXPENSES:
✗ First-class or business-class airfare (unless pre-approved)
✗ Luxury hotels (exceeding per diem rates)
✗ Rental car upgrades (luxury or premium vehicles)
✗ Excessive meals or alcohol
✗ In-room movies or mini-bar charges

PROHIBITED ITEMS:
✗ Alcohol purchases (except client entertainment)
✗ Tobacco products
✗ Cannabis products
✗ Weapons or ammunition
✗ Gambling or lottery tickets
✗ Political donations
✗ Charitable donations (use company program instead)

FINES AND PENALTIES:
✗ Traffic tickets or parking violations
✗ Late fees (credit card, bills)
✗ Penalties for policy violations

EXPENSES WITHOUT PROPER DOCUMENTATION:
✗ Missing receipts
✗ Incomplete expense reports
✗ Expenses submitted after 90-day deadline

4. SPENDING LIMITS AND AUTHORIZATION
================================================================================

4.1 Per Diem Rates

MEALS (per day while traveling):
- Breakfast: $15
- Lunch: $20
- Dinner: $35
- Total daily meal limit: $70

Actual receipts not required for meals under per diem, but must be reasonable.
Exceeding per diem requires manager approval and receipts.

LODGING (per night):
- Tier 1 cities (NYC, SF, LA, Seattle): $250
- Tier 2 cities (Chicago, Boston, Austin): $200
- Tier 3 cities (other major cities): $150
- Tier 4 (small cities/towns): $100

Exceeding lodging limits requires VP approval.

4.2 Authorization Requirements

PRE-APPROVAL REQUIRED:
- Travel exceeding $1,000
- Conference attendance
- Client entertainment over $100
- Technology purchases over $200
- Any expense over $500

MANAGER APPROVAL:
- Up to $2,500 per expense

DIRECTOR APPROVAL:
- $2,501 - $10,000 per expense

VP APPROVAL:
- Over $10,000 per expense

4.3 Corporate Card Limits

Corporate credit card limits:
- Individual Contributors: $5,000/month
- Managers: $10,000/month
- Directors: $25,000/month
- VPs and above: $50,000/month

Exceeding limits requires Finance approval.

5. SUBMISSION PROCESS
================================================================================

5.1 Expense Reporting System

All expenses must be submitted through Concur Expense:
https://concur.techcorp.com

Step-by-Step Process:
1. Log in to Concur
2. Create new expense report
3. Attach receipts (photo or PDF)
4. Categorize each expense
5. Add business purpose description
6. Submit for approval
7. Track status in Concur

5.2 Submission Deadlines

Expenses must be submitted:
- Within 30 days of incurrence (preferred)
- No later than 90 days of incurrence (hard deadline)
- Before the 10th of the month following travel

Late submissions require written justification and may be denied.

5.3 Monthly Reconciliation

Corporate card holders must:
- Reconcile all charges monthly
- Submit expense reports by month-end
- Resolve discrepancies within 5 business days
- Failure to reconcile may result in card suspension

6. REQUIRED DOCUMENTATION
================================================================================

6.1 Receipt Requirements

RECEIPTS REQUIRED for:
- All expenses over $25
- All hotel charges (regardless of amount)
- All airfare
- All rental cars
- Client entertainment (any amount)

RECEIPT NOT REQUIRED for:
- Parking meters under $25
- Public transportation under $25
- Per diem meals under daily limit

6.2 Receipt Format

Acceptable receipts must show:
✓ Merchant name and location
✓ Date of purchase
✓ Itemized list of goods/services
✓ Total amount paid
✓ Payment method

Acceptable formats:
- Original receipt (photo)
- Email receipt (PDF)
- Digital receipt (screenshot)

NOT acceptable:
✗ Credit card statements alone
✗ Handwritten notes
✗ Illegible receipts
✗ Altered receipts

6.3 Lost Receipt Procedure

If receipt is lost:
1. Submit Lost Receipt Affidavit in Concur
2. Provide credit card statement
3. Write detailed explanation
4. Manager must approve
5. Limited to 3 lost receipts per year

6.4 Additional Documentation

For certain expenses, also provide:

TRAVEL:
- Trip itinerary
- Business purpose
- Names of attendees

CLIENT ENTERTAINMENT:
- Names and companies of attendees
- Business purpose
- Agenda or meeting notes

CONFERENCE:
- Conference agenda
- Registration confirmation
- Business justification

MILEAGE:
- Start and end locations
- Odometer readings
- Google Maps distance (for verification)

7. APPROVAL AND PAYMENT TIMELINE
================================================================================

7.1 Approval Workflow

1. Employee submits expense report
2. Manager reviews and approves (2 business days)
3. Finance reviews for policy compliance (2 business days)
4. Payment processed

7.2 Payment Timeline

DIRECT DEPOSIT (recommended):
- Processed every Friday
- Funds available within 2 business days
- Must have direct deposit enrolled

CHECK:
- Processed monthly (1st and 15th)
- Mailed to address on file
- Allow 7-10 business days

CORPORATE CARD PAYMENT:
- Automatically paid from report approval
- Appears on next card statement

7.3 Delays and Rejections

Reasons for delay or rejection:
- Missing receipts
- Incomplete information
- Policy violations
- Duplicate submissions
- Missing pre-approvals

Rejected expenses will be returned with explanation.
Resubmit within 10 days with corrections.

9. TRAVEL EXPENSES
================================================================================

9.1 Airfare

Guidelines:
- Book economy class for flights under 5 hours
- Book 14+ days in advance when possible
- Use preferred airlines (United, Delta, American)
- Book through Concur Travel or approved agency

Business class requires:
- VP approval
- Flights over 5 hours international
- Medical necessity (with documentation)

9.2 Ground Transportation

PREFERRED OPTIONS:
1. Public transportation (most economical)
2. Rideshare (Uber, Lyft)
3. Taxi
4. Rental car (if cost-effective)

RENTAL CARS:
- Mid-size or smaller
- Decline insurance (covered by company policy)
- Refuel before return
- Economy or Compact class preferred

PERSONAL VEHICLE:
- Reimbursed at IRS rate: $0.67/mile (2026)
- Must be most economical option
- Insurance coverage: driver's responsibility

9.3 Lodging

Guidelines:
- Stay within per diem rates (see Section 4.1)
- Book through Concur Travel when possible
- Preferred hotels: Marriott, Hilton, Hyatt
- Stay close to business location to minimize transportation

Acceptable hotel expenses:
✓ Room rate
✓ Taxes and fees
✓ Wi-Fi (if not included)
✓ Business calls

NOT reimbursable:
✗ Mini-bar
✗ In-room movies
✗ Spa services
✗ Room service (unless no other dining option)
✗ Late checkout fees

9.4 Meals During Travel

Per Diem Option:
- Receive $70/day without receipts
- No need to itemize
- Available for trips 1+ days

Actual Expense Option:
- Submit actual meal costs with receipts
- Must be reasonable
- Cannot exceed per diem rates without approval

Alcohol:
- Limited to $15/day
- Only with client entertainment or team dinners
- Excessive alcohol will not be reimbursed

9.5 Extended Travel

For trips exceeding 30 days:
- Weekly return home flights may be reimbursed
- Temporary housing stipend available
- Per diem may be adjusted
- Contact HR for extended travel policy

10. CLIENT ENTERTAINMENT
================================================================================

10.1 Guidelines

Client entertainment is permitted for:
- Building client relationships
- Business development
- Contract negotiations
- Project celebrations

Must be:
- Reasonable and appropriate
- Include business discussion
- Documented with business purpose

10.2 Spending Limits

Per person limits (including TechCorp employees):

MEALS:
- Lunch: $50/person
- Dinner: $100/person
- Exceeding limits requires director approval

EVENTS:
- Sporting events: $200/person
- Theatre/concerts: $150/person
- Golf outings: $250/person (including green fees)

All event entertainment requires pre-approval from director.

10.3 Alcohol Policy

Alcohol at client entertainment:
- Reasonable amounts only
- Stay professional
- Never excessive
- Maximum 2-3 drinks per person
- Driver must not consume alcohol

Company will not reimburse alcohol-related incidents (DUI, etc.)

10.4 Required Documentation

For all client entertainment, document:
- Client names and companies
- TechCorp attendees
- Business purpose
- Itemized receipt
- Date and location

Missing information will result in denial.

10.5 Gifts for Clients

Client gifts are permitted:
- Maximum $100/client/year
- Must be business-appropriate
- No cash or cash equivalents
- Pre-approval required over $50
- Must comply with client's gift policy

Popular gift ideas:
- Company swag
- Gift baskets
- Wine/spirits (if appropriate)
- Event tickets (counts toward entertainment limit)

11. FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
================================================================================

Q: Can I use my corporate card for personal emergencies?
A: No. Corporate cards are for business use only. Personal use may result in
   card cancellation and disciplinary action.

Q: What if my expense was denied?
A: Review the denial reason in Concur. Correct the issue and resubmit, or
   contact expenses@techcorp.com for clarification.

Q: How do I get a corporate credit card?
A: Contact Finance at expenses@techcorp.com. Eligibility depends on role and
   travel frequency.

Q: Can I tip service providers (drivers, waitstaff)?
A: Yes, tips are reimbursable. Standard tipping: 15-20% for meals, $2-5 for
   drivers, $2-5/bag for hotel staff.

Q: What if I need to book travel on short notice?
A: Book through Concur Travel. Short-notice bookings may exceed normal rates;
   provide justification in expense report.

Q: Can I bring my spouse/partner on a business trip?
A: Personal travel is not reimbursable. Any incremental costs (larger hotel
   room, extra meals) are employee's responsibility.

Q: What if the conference hotel is more expensive than per diem?
A: Conference hotels are often exempt from per diem if staying at the event
   venue is required. Get pre-approval from your manager.

Q: How do I expense a team meal or office party?
A: Use "Team Building" expense category. Requires manager approval.
   Limit: $50/person/quarter.

Q: Can I earn credit card points on corporate card?
A: Yes, but company retains rights to points. Personal benefit is incidental.

Q: What if I forgot to expense something from 6 months ago?
A: Expenses must be submitted within 90 days. Exceptions are rare and require
   VP approval with strong justification.
